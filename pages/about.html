So who are we?

We know who we are, but maybe you want to know too...

We are the University of Texas at Austin chapter of the Society of Physics Students or SPS. We are a student run organization that brings together Physics and Non-Physics students here at UT Austin to promote collaboration and circulate information between students. We are the major community building organization for Physics majors campus. Our regular meetings involve anything from professors talking about their research, students talking about something they find interesting, or organizing our future activities. For those of you not convinced, all of our meetings have free pizza.

SPS is also responsible for all of the wonderful Physics shirts you will see around campus. Each semester we take new ideas. So if you ever wanted to be the designer of an iconic piece of UT Physics apparel, you should definitely poke around a meeting when it is shirt sales time. We are always accepting t-shirt ideas even if it is not time for producing t-shirts. If you want to submit an idea then you can follow these instructions.

If in case you are not able to come to our meetings but still want to meet and talk to Physics majors at UT we definitely encourage you to visit the Physics Undergraduate Lounge in RLM 5.222. SPS helps to maintain this room and rents the lockers out to those who want the convenient storage. If you want a locker, fill out one of the forms (found here) and put $15 for a year or $10 for a semester in and envelope. Put this envelope in Locker 4 (the officer locker) and you can use the locker you filled out on the form.

The Society of Physics Students hosts all sorts of community building events. Just to name a few, there are: Minecraft, Intramural Sports, Mentoring, Sunday Study Sessions, Explore UT, Prospie Day, and many more. If you want to get involved in these events or maybe start a new one contact the SPS Officers.

We have no dues to be part of the Society of Physics Students. There is a national organization however that does requre dues. If you are so inclined you can become part of the National SPS here. You do not have to be part of the national organization to come to and enjoy the full extent of our meetings.

Check our Public Calendar for meeting dates and locations. For Fall 2016, the meetings will be: Thrusdays at 6:30-7:30 P.M in RLM 5.104
