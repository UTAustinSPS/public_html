Officers

President: Evan Ott

Hello! I'm Evan and I'm a physicist [crowd, in unison: "Hi Evan"]. I'm a third year physics and computer science major, and have been honored to serve as SPS president for the past year. I'm really excited about all the new things we started this past year (student workshops, student research presentations at our weekly meetings, and mentoring groups) and can't wait to see how they evolve this year! And with the renewal of our partner organization, Sigma Pi Sigma, the physics honors society, I look forward to bright future of UT Physics! Like several of the other officers, you can frequently find me in the Undergraduate Physics Lounge in RLM!

Vice President: Gabriel Cossio

YO! I am Gabriel Cossio and I am currently a fourth year physics major at UT. I have been doing research for all of my four years from things ranging from cluster fusion experiments and simulations of the early universe with the Texas Petawatt Laser system to the study of light matter interactions to discover the quantum behavior of solids. This will mark my third year as an officer for SPS and I loved every moment of it. SPS is a great place to learn about the cutting edge physics that his happening on campus and around the world and meet people who want join you in your efforts in understanding all of it. If you are interested in the outdoors, frisbee, quantum physics, bad jokes, free pizza, and Carl Sagan autotunez SPS is the place for you.

Outreach Coordinator: Courtney Campion

Hello! My name is Courtney Campion, and I am a senior physics major aspiring to go to law school! I am greatly looking forward to getting SPS in the community, and more active as a club. I like physics, volcanoes and the color purple. I am often found in RLM and love doing outdoor things, and thus I am anxious to get the physics club outside and involved in more sport related adventures! Feel free to talk to me about any questions you have about physics, or in general!

Treasurer: Danaan Thome

After spending my early years in Austin, I spent a considerable portion of my youth growing up on a ten−acre plot of loblolly pines outside of Smithville, TX. Living in a collection of tents, campers, and trailers, the house we were building collapsed with us inside of it on St. Patrick's day. We subsequently moved back to Austin. After that, when I was eleven, I was nearly trampled to death by a stampede of cows and have been enacting by vengeance over the course of a lifetime−by steak or by hamburger. After the move, in middle school, I found my dad's electronics kits in the attic and began tinkering, learning about how the things around me can work predictably and with precision; I have been pursuing my interest in science−though not unwaveringly−ever since. Now, I'm a jth year math, physics, and biology student and (currently) keep myself busy with the preliminary stages of starting a game company.
