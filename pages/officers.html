Officers

President: Evan Ott

Hello! I'm Evan and I'm a physicist [crowd, in unison: "Hi Evan"]. I'm a third year physics and computer science major, and have been honored to serve as SPS president for the past year. I'm really excited about all the new things we started this past year (student workshops, student research presentations at our weekly meetings, and mentoring groups) and can't wait to see how they evolve this year! And with the renewal of our partner organization, Sigma Pi Sigma, the physics honors society, I look forward to bright future of UT Physics! Like several of the other officers, you can frequently find me in the Undergraduate Physics Lounge in RLM!

Vice President: Tharon Morrison

Hi everyone. My Name is Tharon Morrison. I am a Junior and work in the Raizen Lab on the Magnetic Focusing experiment. I really enjoy working for SPS and seeing you all progress. Projects that I work on within SPS include the mentoring groups and the website. I am always available to converse (about anything, even LoL) and my favorite saying about physicists (that definitely applies to me) is: "Physicists have a lot to say, they are only waiting for an excuse to start talking." If you have questions about anything Physics or Sigma Pi Sigma, I can either answer it or know who has the answer.

Treasurer: Jason Koeller

My name is Jason Koeller, and I am a fourth-year physics major. Originally from Houston, I transferred to UT from Colorado School of Mines after my sophomore year. I first encountered physics as a junior in high school, and realized that I really enjoyed it when I found myself spending free time watching physics lectures online. In high school I was also an enthusiastic electronics tinkerer, and for a brief period I built and sold high-powered handheld lasers. After two years in Colorado as an avid member of the ski team I am now dedicated to physics and physics-related things (SPS and Sigma Pi Sigma!), working on a theory project involving dark matter with Prof. Kilic. My plan for the (near) future is to attend graduate school in high-energy theory after I graduate from UT this summer. If you have any questions about physics, electronics, Colorado, or life in general, feel free to ask!

Secretary: Victoria Levy

My name is Victoria and I am a second year physics major from New York. I am currently a mentor for Women in Natural Sciences as well as an LA for Dr. Markert's Mechanics class. Last year I was part of the Electronics and Magnetic Materials Research FRI Stream, and I am now working in Dr. Heinzen's lab on finding the electron electric dipole moment. I love the SPS community and I am really looking forward to this year. Also, I like cats and mountains.

Outreach Coordinator: Courtney Campion

Hello! My name is Courtney Campion, and I am a senior physics major aspiring to go to law school! I am greatly looking forward to getting SPS in the community, and more active as a club.
